MITIGATION

Mitigation refers to strategies and actions aimed at reducing or preventing greenhouse gas emissions to slow down global warming. It addresses the root causes of climate change by promoting clean energy, energy efficiency, forest protection, and sustainable technologies. Effective mitigation helps protect ecosystems, reduce pollution, and build a safer, more resilient future for all.

Renewable Energy
  • Solar, wind, hydropower
  • Reduces carbon emissions
  • Supports sustainable electricity
Energy Efficiency
  • Efficient appliances & buildings
  • Reduces wasted energy
  • Lowers emissions and costs
Forest & Nature Protection
  • Reforestation & afforestation
  • Protects biodiversity
  • Absorbs CO₂ naturally
Sustainable Transport
  • Electric vehicles & public transport
  • Promotes walking & cycling
  • Reduces emissions
Waste Reduction
  • Recycling & composting
  • Reduces landfill pollution
  • Supports circular economy
Carbon Capture Technology
  • Captures industrial CO₂
  • Prevents emissions from entering atmosphere
  • Supports low-carbon future

GLOBAL POLICY

Global Policy focuses on international agreements, cooperation, and actions taken by countries to address global issues such as climate change, security, public health, and sustainable development. It involves treaties, diplomacy, and coordinated strategies that ensure nations work together toward a safer and more sustainable world.

Paris Agreement
  • Global treaty to reduce emissions
  • Limit warming to 1.5°C
  • Signed by 190+ countries
United Nations Climate Action
  • Global cooperation on climate goals
  • Supports countries in sustainability
  • Implements SDG 13 (Climate Action)
International Agreements
  • Countries unite for global solutions
  • Promotes sustainable development
  • Ensures shared responsibilities
Climate Finance
  • Funds for climate adaptation & mitigation
  • Supports vulnerable countries
  • Promotes clean energy investments
International Cooperation
  • Nations working together
  • Shared environmental commitments
  • Creates unified global action
Policy Enforcement
  • Ensures rules are followed
  • Improves accountability
  • Strengthens climate action

THE FIVE P's OF GLOBAL POLICY

The Five P's represent the main tools governments use to guide public behavior, manage resources, and strengthen international policy. These include Prescriptive Regulation, Property Rights, Payments, Penalties, and Persuasion — all working together to promote sustainable and responsible global action.

Prescriptive Regulation
  • Clear rules and required standards
  • Ensures environmental protection
  • Supports safety and compliance
Property Rights
  • Legal ownership of resources
  • Prevents overuse and misuse
  • Encourages responsible management
Payments
  • Financial incentives for action
  • Promotes clean and green practices
  • Supports vulnerable communities
Penalties
  • Fines for harmful actions
  • Discourages environmental damage
  • Strengthens policy enforcement
Persuasion
  • Education and awareness campaigns
  • Encourages voluntary behavior change
  • Promotes sustainable choices

Adaptation & Resilience

Adaptation and resilience are essential for building strong communities that can withstand the impacts of climate change and other disruptions. By planning ahead and implementing effective strategies, communities can protect lives, secure resources, support economic stability, and maintain vital ecosystems. Adaptation ensures that people and infrastructures remain safe, while resilience strengthens a community’s ability to recover, rebuild, and thrive even after major challenges.

Protecting Lives and Property

Early warning systems, emergency preparedness, and resilient planning reduce casualties and damage during climate-related disasters.

Enhancing Economic Stability

Climate-resilient infrastructure lowers long-term recovery costs. Every $1 invested in resilience can save over $13 in rebuilding expenses.

Securing Food and Water Systems

Drought-resistant crops, climate-smart agriculture, and rainwater harvesting ensure continued access to essential resources during extreme weather.

Building Climate-Resilient Infrastructure

Designing power, water, and transportation systems with future climate risks in mind prevents costly service disruptions.

Fostering Social Cohesion

Community-led resilience planning builds stronger social bonds, empowers local voices, and ensures solutions fit the community’s needs.

Supporting Policy and Planning

Local resilience plans and national adaptation strategies help guide investments, align government efforts, and provide long-term roadmaps for action.

Individual & Local Actions

Everyone can help address local and global challenges through individual actions and community involvement, which collectively build momentum for systemic change. Local knowledge is essential for creating effective, context-specific solutions for a range of issues, from environmental protection to social well-being.

Individual Actions for a Healthy Planet

Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Cut down on waste, compost food scraps, and recycle paper, glass, plastic, and electronics.

Conserve Energy

Use energy-efficient bulbs, turn off lights, and unplug electronics when not in use.

Choose Sustainable Transport

Walk, bike, or use public transport to reduce CO2 emissions; save car trips for when necessary.

Eat Wisely

Consider a plant-based diet and make smart seafood choices using resources like Fishwatch.gov.

Shop Locally & Wisely

Support neighborhood businesses, use reusable bags, and buy fewer new items.

Conserve Water

Fix leaky faucets and reduce water usage to protect local waterways.

Avoid Toxic Chemicals

Choose non-toxic products at home and work to prevent pollution of water and soil.

Local Actions for Stronger Communities

Volunteer

Participate in park cleanups, food banks, or educational programs. Use platforms like Idealist or VolunteerMatch.

Plant Trees

Organize tree-planting events to clean the air, save energy, and combat climate change.

Advocate for Green Policies

Attend city council meetings, sign petitions, and push for sustainability policies.

Support Local Initiatives

Start or contribute to community gardens to provide fresh food and foster connection.

Promote Safety

Advocate for better lighting, crosswalks, and speed bumps to protect pedestrians.

Emergency Preparedness

Join local disaster response teams like CERT to prepare for and respond to natural disasters.

Stay Informed

Elect leaders who support sustainable and equitable community development.
